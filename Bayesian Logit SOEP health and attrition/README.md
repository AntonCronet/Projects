# Health Determinants of Panel Attrition: A SOEP Logistic Regression Analysis

## Project Overview

This project investigates the relationship between health factors and panel attrition in the German Socio-Economic Panel (SOEP). The analysis focuses on whether physical and mental health measures predict the likelihood of survey dropout in subsequent waves.

The project compares multiple logistic regression specifications and re-estimates the preferred model using Bayesian methods to obtain more interpretable uncertainty estimates and predicted probabilities.

The main finding is that mental health is the only health dimension that remains a significant predictor of attrition after controlling for demographic, socioeconomic, and survey-related characteristics.

## Data

The analysis uses data from the German Socio-Economic Panel (SOEP).

Key variables include:

### Health Variables

* SF-12 Mental Component Summary (MCS)
* SF-12 Physical Component Summary (PCS)
* Physical Functioning
* Role Physical
* Bodily Pain
* General Health
* Vitality
* Social Functioning
* Role Emotional
* Mental Health
* Body Mass Index (BMI)
* Grip Strength

### Control Variables

* Age
* Gender
* Employment Status
* Migration Background
* Partner Status
* Weekly Working Hours
* Years of Education
* Interview Mode
* SOEP Sample
* Survey Year

### Outcome Variable

Attrition is defined as permanent non-participation in the following survey wave, excluding cases where non-response is caused by death.

## Analysis Workflow

### 1. Data Preparation

Run:

```r
data.Rmd
```

This script:

* Loads SOEP data
* Constructs the attrition variable
* Creates health indicators
* Applies variable transformations
* Generates the analysis samples
* Exports cleaned datasets

Output:


## Main Results

Key findings include:

* Mental health is the only health dimension that consistently predicts attrition after controls are included.
* A one standard deviation improvement in mental health reduces attrition odds by approximately 8%.
* Physical health measures, BMI, and grip strength are not significant in the fully specified models.
* Individuals with a migration background show higher attrition rates.
* Education and age reduce attrition risk.
